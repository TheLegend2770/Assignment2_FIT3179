History of Cheese

Cheese making can be traced back almost 10,000 years ago, with evidence of countries developing cheese in their own ways. All countries have cheeses unique to their regions. The famous Green County Cheese factory in America, is known to produce 200 different types of cheese. (National Historic Cheese Making Center, https://nationalhistoriccheesemakingcenter.org/history-of-cheese/)

What Makes the Cheese Taste Good?

Figures 5, 6 and 7 show the possible reasons for which the award winning cheeses have a superior taste and quality. By testing the impact of acetic acid (acetic), hydrogen sulfide (H2S) and lactic levels in different cheeses. Overall, H2S seems to be the best predictor for cheese taste, with higher levels of H2S suggesting superior cheese taste. This is the same with lactic levels, with a slight correlation between lactic concentration and higher cheese taste. These levels suggest that Spain, as well as other European countries have found a way to use these concentrations to their advantage and produce high quality cheeses.
